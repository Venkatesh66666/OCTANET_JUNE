Gado Gado

Gado-gado from East Java is a traditional Indonesian dish that is easy and quick to make. This time, Kadek Arini made it in the Endeus kitchen! Kadek also used Sasa Santan in the peanut sauce. Sasa Santan contains the benefits of Omega 3, making the dish not only delicious but also healthy!

Ingredients

  • 2 carrots, cut into 3 cm rectangular pieces
  • 50 g long beans, cut into 3 cm pieces
  • 50 g cabbage, sliced into 1 cm pieces
  • 50 g curly lettuce, torn into pieces
  • 1 potato, boiled, cut into 8 pieces
  • 1 cucumber, sliced into rounds
  • 1 tomato, cut into 6 pieces
  • 250 g peanuts, roasted, ground
  • 200 ml Sasa liquid coconut milk
  • 200 ml water
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 4 red bird's eye chilies
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves, stems removed
  • 3 tbsp palm sugar, shaved
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp tamarind, dissolved in 3 tbsp water
  • 2 rice cakes (lontong)
  • 2 chicken eggs
  • 2 pieces of white tofu, fried, diced
  • 2 pieces of tempeh, fried, diced
  • onion crackers



How to Make

  • Boil sequentially cabbage, carrots, and green beans. Drain. Set aside.
  • Peanut sauce: In a blender, blend peanuts, garlic, bird's eye chili, kaffir lime leaves, brown sugar, salt, tamarind juice, and water. Pour in coconut milk (Sasa Santan) into the blender. Blend until smooth and well combined.
  • Pour the sauce into a saucepan. Cook while stirring until well combined. Bring to a boil. Remove from heat. Let it cool.
  • Arrange boiled vegetables and garnishes, lettuce leaves, cucumber, and tomatoes on a serving plate. Pour peanut sauce over. Serve immediately.
